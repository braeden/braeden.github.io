About Me

Braeden Smith

Hey, my name is Braeden, I’m a graduate of Staples High School and I’m currently a freshman at the University of Illinois, majoring in Computer Engineering. I’m extremely passionate about programming and FOSS, and I have experience in a variety of languages, including: Python, Java, Bash, Ruby, and JS/jQuery. For the most part I produce random scripts (as you can see on my github page), but I’ve also contributed to projects like Betaflight and Clover. I also have lots of experience with Linux based OSs and Git. Beyond that, I dabble a bit in network security, including XSS and access flaws.

Throughout high school, I spent 15-40 hrs/wk working as a LD/ME/VP Tech for Staples Players. Staples Players produces an extremely high caliber of theater with incredible community support, in fact, we ended up being 1 of 5 high schools selected by Disney Theatrical to test Newsies! I’ve worked on an assortment of different styles during more than 15 productions, from the Laramie Project, to Urinetown, to Newsies, to 12 Angry Men, and more - I’ve received 2 awards for my immense technical contribution. See some of our productions here.

I’ve interned at two start-up companies, Saphlux out of Yale University, who focused on a changing GaN LED production and Zenabi Data, who focuses on machine learning and big data analysis.
In my free-time, for fun, I love to building and flying FPV racing drones.
